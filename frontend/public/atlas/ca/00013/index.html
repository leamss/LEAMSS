About this occupation

Senior managers in health, education, social and community services and membership organizations plan, organize, direct, control and evaluate, through middle managers, membership and other organizations or institutions that deliver health, education, social or community services. They formulate policies which establish the direction to be taken by these organizations, either alone or in conjunction with a board of directors. They are employed in health care organizations, educational services, social and community services and membership organizations or they may own and operate their own business.

Typical tasks

  1. This group performs some or all of the following duties:
  2. Establish objectives for the organization or institution and formulate or approve policies and programs
  3. Authorize and organize the establishment of major departments and associated senior staff positions
  4. Allocate material, human and financial resources to implement organizational policies and programs; establish financial and administrative controls; formulate and approve promotional campaigns; and approve overall personnel planning
  5. Select middle managers, directors or other executive staff
  6. Coordinate the work of regions, divisions or departments
  7. Represent the organization, or delegate representatives to act on behalf of the organization, in negotiations or other official functions.
  8. Senior managers in this unit group may specialize in areas such as finance, marketing, human resources or in a particular service area.
