About this occupation

Respiratory therapists assist physicians in the evaluation, diagnosis, treatment and care of patients with respiratory and cardiopulmonary disorders. Clinical perfusionists provide technical support to patients undergoing cardiac surgery and patients requiring cardio-respiratory support. Cardiopulmonary technologists assist physicians in the technical aspects of diagnosis and treatment of cardiovascular and pulmonary disease. Respiratory therapists are employed in hospitals, extended care facilities, public health centres, sleep, private or dental clinics and respiratory home care companies. Clinical perfusionists and cardiopulmonary technologists are primarily employed in hospitals. Respiratory therapists, clinical perfusionists and cardiopulmonary technologists who are supervisors or instructors are included in this unit group.

Typical tasks

  1. This group performs some or all of the following duties:
  2. Respiratory therapists
  3. Perform diagnostic tests, such as arterial blood gas analysis and cardiopulmonary functions tests
  4. Operate and monitor respiratory equipment to administer treatments such as oxygen, oxygen-air mixtures, humidified air or specialty medical gases
  5. Operate, monitor, maintain and test a variety of diagnostic and therapeutic equipment
  6. Perform or assist with interventions such as airway maintenance, line insertions, inductions and intubations, and manage patients and associated equipment
  7. Execute anesthesia assistance services
  8. Perform artificial respiration and external cardiac massage
  9. Evaluate and monitor patients' cardiorespiratory conditions
  10. Provide and optimize patients' mechanical ventilation
  11. Assist with transport of high-risk patients
  12. May prepare and administer authorized medications
  13. Supervise and train students and other respiratory therapists
  14. Participate in home care programs for chronic respiratory patients and provide patient and family education
  15. Participate in research related to cardiac and pulmonary disorders.
  16. Respiratory therapists may work in specific areas of practice such as anesthesia, critical care, neonatal and pediatrics, cardiopulmonary diagnostics, respiratory home and community care, research and private health industry.
  17. Clinical perfusionists
  18. Assemble, maintain and operate extracorporeal circulation equipment, intra-aortic balloon pumps and other heart assist devices to support or temporarily replace patients' cardiopulmonary functions during open-heart surgery
  19. Administer blood products, drugs and other substances through heart-lung machines and other devices as directed by cardiac surgeons and anaesthetists to maintain adequate flow of oxygenated blood to all organs of the body
  20. Monitor vital signs to support and maintain patients' physiological functions and metabolic needs during cardiopulmonary surgery
  21. Participate in routine maintenance, calibration and inspection of all perfusion related equipment
  22. Supervise and train student clinical perfusionists and other clinical perfusionists.
  23. Cardiopulmonary technologists
  24. Perform diagnostic tests, such as pulmonary function and asthma stress, or assist physicians with cardiac and cardiopulmonary stress tests and bronchoscopies
  25. Determine patients' blood characteristics such as activated clotting time and oxygen saturation
