About this occupation

Physician assistants and midwives provide primary health care and treatment in conjunction with physicians and in collaboration with other health professionals. Physician assistants provide a range of health services to patients delivering preventive and continuous care toward the management of patients' health. Midwives provide full-course care to women and their babies during the pre- and post-natal period. This unit group also includes professionals such as genetic counsellors, orthoptists and pathologists' assistants, who work in collaboration with primary health occupations. Genetic counsellors assess individual or family risk of genetic conditions, and provide information to individuals, families and healthcare providers to promote informed choice. Orthoptists diagnose and treat binocular disorders. Pathologists' assistants assist at autopsies and examinations of surgical specimens, or perform autopsies under a pathologist's supervision. Physician assistants usually work under physicians in private practice, including group or team practices, hospitals and clinics. Midwives are employed in hospitals, clinics, birthing centres or in private practice. Genetic counsellors usually work in private practice, hospitals, clinics and research institutes. Orthoptists work under an ophthalmologist, particularly in private practices or clinics. Pathologists' assistants are usually employed in hospitals and universities.

Typical tasks

  1. This group performs some or all of the following duties:
  2. Physician assistants
  3. Conduct patient interviews, physical assessments and take medical histories
  4. Perform or order diagnostic tests and interpret results
  5. In consultation with a supervising physician, formulate and implement treatment plans and monitor patients' progress
  6. Perform and assist in minor surgery
  7. Provide health maintenance education
  8. May participate in research activities
  9. May prescribe from a limited list of medications.
  10. Midwives
  11. Provide primary, full-course, care to women and their babies during pregnancy, such as physical examinations, screening and diagnostic tests to monitor the health of mother and baby and make referrals to medical specialists as required
  12. Manage labour and spontaneous normal deliveries
  13. Care for, assess and monitor the health of newborns and refer infants to appropriate medical services as required
  14. Provide advice and guidance regarding care for newborns and young infants.
  15. Genetic counsellors
  16. Provide information and support to families affected by or at risk of a genetic disorder
  17. Gather and analyze family history and inheritance patterns, calculate risks of recurrence, and provide information about genetic testing and related procedures
  18. Develop, implement or coordinate action plans and refer individuals and families to other health professionals
  19. Design and conduct genetics training programs for other healthcare professionals or the general public
  20. May conduct research in the field of medical genetics and genetic counselling.
  21. Orthoptists
  22. Work alongside ophthalmologists to diagnose and treat eye disorders by performing specialized eye tests to measure and assess defective binocular vision or abnormal eye movement in patients
  23. Prescribe treatment such as eye exercises or patching regimens.
  24. Pathologists' assistants
  25. Prepare for autopsies by obtaining patients' medical records and arranging for radiographic examinations
