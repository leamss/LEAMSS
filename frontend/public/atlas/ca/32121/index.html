About this occupation

Medical radiation technologists operate radiographic and radiation therapy equipment to administer radiation treatment and produce images of body structures for the diagnosis and treatment of injury and disease. They are employed in hospitals, cancer treatment centres, clinics, radiological laboratories, colleges and universities. Medical radiation technologists who are supervisors or instructors are included in this unit group.

Typical tasks

  1. This group performs some or all of the following duties:
  2. Radiological technologists
  3. Operate X-ray, radiographic and fluoroscopic equipment, computerized tomography (CT) scanners, mammography units and magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) scanners to produce radiographs or anatomic images of the human body for the diagnosis by radiologists of disease or injury
  4. Record and process patient data
  5. Perform basic verification and quality control checks on radiographic and film processing equipment
  6. Provide appropriate care and monitoring of the patient during the radiographic examination
  7. Explain procedures, position patient and equipment and apply radiation protection measures
  8. May train and supervise student radiographers or supervise other radiological technologists.
  9. Radiological technologists may specialize in areas such as computerized tomography, angiography, mammography, magnetic resonance imaging, interventional radiology, dosimetry, stereotaxy or brachytherapy.
  10. Nuclear medicine technologists
  11. Prepare and administer radiopharmaceuticals, such as radionuclides and other tracer materials to patients or to biological samples
  12. Operate radiation detection equipment, such as gamma cameras, scanners, scintillation counters, tomodensitometers and ionization chambers, to acquire data for use by nuclear medicine physicians in the diagnosis of disease
  13. Perform diagnostic procedures using radioactive materials on biological specimens, such as blood, urine and faeces
  14. Record and process results of procedures
  15. Check equipment to ensure proper operation
  16. Provide appropriate care and monitoring of the patient during the examination
  17. Apply radiation protection measures
  18. May train and supervise student nuclear medicine technologists or supervise other nuclear medicine technologists.
  19. Radiation therapists
  20. Operate linear accelerators, cobalt 60, X-ray and other radiation therapy equipment to administer radiation treatment prescribed by radiation oncologists
  21. Check radiation therapy equipment to ensure proper operation
  22. Assist radiation oncologists and clinical physicists with preparation of radiation treatment plan
  23. Assist in the preparation of sealed radioactive materials such as cobalt, radium, cesium and isotopes and the construction of devices such as plaster casts and acrylic moulds to assist with administration of radiation treatment
  24. Provide appropriate care and monitoring of the patient's physical and psychological well-being during the entire course of treatment
  25. Explain procedures and side effects of radiation
