Typical tasks

  1. This group performs some or all of the following duties:
  2. Plan, develop, implement and evaluate human resources and labour relations strategies including policies, programs and procedures to address an organization's human resource requirements
  3. Advise employers and employees on the interpretation of human resources policies, compensation and benefit programs and collective agreements
  4. Negotiate collective agreements on behalf of employers or workers, mediate labour disputes and grievances and provide advice on employee and labour relations
  5. Research and prepare occupational classifications, job descriptions, salary scales and competency appraisal measures and systems
  6. Plan and administer staffing, total compensation, training and career development, employee assistance, employment equity and affirmative action programs
  7. Manage programs and maintain human resources information and related records systems
  8. Hire and oversee training of staff
  9. Coordinate employee performance appraisal programs
  10. Research employee benefit and health and safety practices and recommend changes or modifications to existing policies.

Qualification rules

In Canada, human resources professionals under NOC 11200 are typically required to hold a bachelor's degree in human resources management, business administration, industrial relations, commerce, or a related field. Some employers may accept a college diploma combined with substantial relevant work experience in lieu of a degree. Completion of a Certified Human Resources Professional (CHRP) or Certified Human Resources Leader (CHRL) designation through the HRPA or equivalent provincial HR association is commonly expected or preferred by employers, though specific requirements vary by province and employer. Relevant work experience in HR functions such as recruitment, compensation, labour relations, or HR policy development is generally required, with the level of experience expected increasing for senior or specialist roles. Candidates seeking immigration pathways should be prepared to demonstrate both their academic credentials and progressive professional experience, as assessing bodies and visa programs typically evaluate both components when determining eligibility.
