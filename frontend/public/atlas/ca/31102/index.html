About this occupation

General practitioners and family physicians diagnose and treat the diseases, physiological disorders and injuries of patients. They provide primary contact and continuous care toward the management of patients' health. They usually work in private practice, including group or team practices, hospitals and clinics. Residents in training to be general practitioners or family physicians are included in this unit group.

Typical tasks

  1. This group performs some or all of the following duties:
  2. Examine patients and take their histories, order laboratory tests, X-rays and other diagnostic procedures and consult with other medical practitioners to evaluate patients' physical and mental health
  3. Prescribe and administer medications and treatments
  4. Perform and assist in routine surgery
  5. Provide emergency care
  6. Provide acute care management
  7. Vaccinate patients to prevent and treat diseases
  8. Deliver babies and provide pre-natal and post-natal care
  9. Advise patients and their families on health care including health promotion, disease, illness and accident prevention
  10. Provide counselling and support to patients and their families on a wide range of health and lifestyle issues
  11. Perform patient advocacy role
  12. Coordinate or manage primary patient care
  13. Provide continuous care to patients
  14. Supervise home care services
  15. Report births, deaths, and contagious and other diseases to governmental authorities.
