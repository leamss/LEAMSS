About this occupation

An Aquaculture Farmer plans, manages, and oversees the cultivation of aquatic organisms—including fish, shellfish, and marine plants—within controlled or semi-controlled environments such as sea cages, ponds, and longline systems. This role encompasses the full production cycle, from stocking and feeding through to harvesting and preparing product for sale, while maintaining compliance with environmental and biosecurity standards. Aquaculture Farmers may operate independently or manage staff on small-to-large scale marine or freshwater farming operations across New Zealand.

Typical tasks

  1. Plan and manage aquatic farming operations including stocking, feeding, and harvesting schedules.
  2. Monitor water quality, temperature, and environmental conditions to optimise stock health.
  3. Inspect and maintain farming equipment such as cages, nets, moorings, and pumping systems.
  4. Implement biosecurity and disease management protocols to protect stock.
  5. Record production data, stock inventories, and operational costs for business management.
  6. Coordinate harvesting activities and prepare product for transport or sale.
  7. Liaise with regulatory authorities to ensure compliance with resource consents and permits.
  8. Supervise, train, and allocate tasks to farm workers and seasonal staff.
  9. Source and procure feed, equipment, and supplies from approved vendors.
  10. Assess and respond to environmental or weather-related risks affecting farm operations.

Qualification rules

There is no single mandatory qualification for Aquaculture Farmers in New Zealand; however, candidates are typically expected to hold a relevant qualification in aquaculture, marine science, agriculture, or a related field—such as a New Zealand Certificate or Diploma in Aquaculture—or to demonstrate substantial practical experience in lieu of formal study. NZQA, as the assessing body under the Federal pathway, will evaluate an applicant's qualifications and skills against New Zealand standards, considering both formal credentials and documented work experience. Applicants should be prepared to provide evidence of hands-on experience in aquaculture operations, including supervisory or management responsibilities where applicable. Overseas qualifications will be assessed for comparability to New Zealand equivalents. Additional industry certifications related to maritime safety, biosecurity, or farm management may strengthen an application, though specific requirements should be confirmed directly with NZQA prior to lodging an assessment.
