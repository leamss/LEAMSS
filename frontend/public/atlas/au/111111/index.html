About this occupation

Chief Executives and Managing Directors are senior leaders responsible for the overall strategic direction, operational performance, and governance of an organisation. They work in close collaboration with boards of directors, executive teams, and key stakeholders to set corporate objectives, allocate resources, and ensure organisational goals are met. This occupation spans a broad range of industries and organisational sizes, requiring demonstrated leadership capability, business acumen, and the ability to drive sustainable growth and change.

Typical tasks

  1. Determine and articulate the organisation's strategic vision, goals, and long-term plans.
  2. Oversee day-to-day operations and ensure alignment with board-approved objectives.
  3. Report to and liaise with the board of directors on organisational performance.
  4. Manage senior leadership teams and delegate responsibilities across business units.
  5. Monitor financial performance, budgets, and risk management frameworks.
  6. Represent the organisation in negotiations with major clients, partners, and government bodies.
  7. Ensure compliance with relevant legislation, regulations, and corporate governance standards.
  8. Lead organisational change initiatives and drive continuous improvement programs.
  9. Approve major capital expenditure, contracts, and policy decisions.
  10. Build and maintain relationships with key external stakeholders and industry bodies.

Qualification rules

Candidates are typically expected to hold a bachelor's degree or higher qualification in business administration, commerce, management, or a closely related discipline. Postgraduate qualifications such as an MBA or equivalent are common and may strengthen an application; however, extensive senior management experience at an executive or equivalent level is generally weighted heavily alongside formal education. The assessing body for this occupation is the Institute of Managers and Leaders National (IML National). Applicants should be prepared to demonstrate a sustained history of executive-level leadership, accountability for organisation-wide outcomes, and evidence that their role involved genuine strategic authority rather than operational management alone. Skills assessment requirements, including documentation standards and experience thresholds, should be confirmed directly with IML National and against current Department of Home Affairs guidelines prior to lodgement.
